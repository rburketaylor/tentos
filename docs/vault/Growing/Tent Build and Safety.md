# Tent Build and Safety

## Tent choice

### Buy a tent (recommended for this build)

- Target size: `4 x 4 x 6.5 ft` (48 x 48 x 80 in).
- **Mars Hydro** preferred: ~$120-170 CAD on Amazon.ca. 1680D canvas, good light-proofing, SBS zippers.
- VIVOSUN is cheaper ($80-130) but thinner canvas and zipper light leaks. The $20-40 difference is worth spending.
- Generic AliExpress tents ($60-100): inconsistent quality, flimsy frames. Not recommended.
- Verify the tent is 80 inches tall before ordering. Some cheaper 4x4 tents are only 60-66 inches.

### DIY enclosure (if preferred)

- PVC frame if cost and easy assembly matter most.
- `2x2` wood frame if you want stronger mounting points.
- Panda film is the best cheap interior choice.
- Black plastic, canvas, blackout curtain, or moving blanket for the exterior.
- A simple flap door with Velcro or similar closure is enough.

## Material guidance

- Panda film is usually easier and more durable than Mylar.
- Mylar is usable, but it creases easily and is more annoying to work with.
- For a bought 4x4 tent, the included reflective mylar interior is usually fine.

## Ventilation design for 4x4

- Tent volume: `4 x 4 x 6.5 = 104 cu ft`.
- 480W LED produces ~100-145W of heat.
- Target ~300+ CFM after carbon filter restriction.
- **6 inch inline fan rated 400-450 CFM** is the right size. Not 4 inch, not 8 inch.
- Passive intake (bottom port with mesh screen) + active exhaust (top-rear).
- Carbon filter hangs inside tent, connected directly to inline fan.
- Tent walls should bow inward slightly when running = negative pressure confirmed.
- Use 6 inch insulated flex duct. Keep runs short with gentle curves.
- Seal connections with foil tape and duct clamps.

## Temperature management

- **Cooling is the primary concern** with 480W LED.
- Expect 3-10F above ambient during lights-on with exhaust running.
- Run lights at night (e.g., 8PM-8AM) to take advantage of cooler ambient temps.
- Supplemental heat only needed if room drops below 16C during lights-off in winter.
- If heat is needed: small oil-filled radiator with temp controller or seedling heat mat.

## Safety priorities

- Modern LED grows are mostly electrical risk, not HID-style heat risk.
- Secure the light so it cannot fall onto fabric or film.
- Mount the LED driver outside the tent where it gets airflow (hottest and most humidity-sensitive component).
- Keep reflective film and plastic away from the fixture heatsink and wiring.
- Avoid overloaded power strips, bad extension cords, and questionable drivers.
- If using a Mean Well ELG-480-54A, ensure it is genuine (counterfeits exist). Under $40 for 480W is a fake.
- Maintain airflow even in a DIY enclosure.

## Light guidance for 4x4

- DIY LM301B quantum board build is the best value: ~$191-284 CAD vs $450-550 for commercial equivalent.
- Two 240W boards spread across the canopy gives good coverage for a 4x4 footprint.
- 3000K for flowering focus, 3500K for full-cycle (veg + flower compromise).
- Supplement with 660nm deep red (essential), 730nm far red (beneficial), 395nm UV-A (late flower only).
- Avoid fake wattage marketing and old blurple fixtures.
- Bar or segmented lights matter more once the footprint gets larger than 4x4.
