# Coco Coir Grow Guide

## Why coco coir

- Inert medium: no nutrients in the substrate, you control everything.
- High air-to-water ratio: roots get oxygen even when wet. Less overwatering risk than soil.
- Fast drainage: enables frequent fertigation without waterlogging.
- Reusable 2-3 times if properly rinsed and buffered between cycles.
- Works with standard hydroponic nutrient schedules.

## Coco coir properties

- **Cation exchange capacity (CEC)**: Coco holds positively charged ions (Ca, Mg, K, Na). If not buffered, it will rob these from your nutrient solution.
- **pH**: Naturally slightly acidic (~5.5-6.5). Must adjust to 5.8-6.2 for nutrient availability.
- **Salt content**: Unbuffered coco often contains high sodium and potassium from processing. Must rinse and buffer.

## Buffering procedure (not optional)

Unbuffered coco will lock out Ca and Mg, causing deficiencies that look like nutrient problems but are actually a medium problem.

1. Hydrate bricks in a large container with warm water.
2. Rinse 2-3 times with pH-adjusted water (pH 5.5-6.0) to remove excess sodium and potassium.
3. Soak in CalMag solution: **1 tsp CalMag per gallon of water**, pH adjusted to 5.5-6.0.
4. Let soak for 4-8 hours (overnight is fine).
5. Rinse once more with pH-adjusted water.
6. Drain and fluff. Final pH should be 5.5-6.0.
7. Mix with perlite at 70/30 coco/perlite ratio for improved drainage and aeration.

## Nutrient stack

### Base nutrients

| Product | NPK | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Mega Crop Part A (5-12-26) | 5-12-26 | P, K, sulfur, micronutrients |
| Mega Crop Part B (15.5-0-0) | 15.5-0-0 | N and Ca (calcium nitrate) |

### Supplements

| Product | Purpose | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| CalMag | Ca + Mg supplementation | Every feeding in coco. Mandatory. |
| MKP (0-52-34) | Bloom booster (P + K) | Early to mid flower. |
| K2SO4 (potassium sulfate) | Late flower K push without extra P or N | Mid to late flower. |
| Epsom Salt (MgSO4) | Mg supplementation for heavy K | As needed, especially late flower. |
| pH Down | Lower pH to 5.8-6.2 | Every mixing. Usually needed. |
| pH Up | Raise pH | Rarely needed in this setup. Manual only. |

### What you do NOT need

- **Silica**: Nice for cell wall strength under high light, but not essential. Add later if wanted.
- **Humic/Fulvic acid**: Minimal benefit in drain-to-waste coco. Skip.
- **Enzymes (Cannazym etc)**: More important for recirculating. Drain-to-waste does not accumulate dead roots. Skip.
- **Beneficial microbes**: Nice but not essential. Skip for v1.
- **Bud boosters**: MKP + K2SO4 already cover what commercial bud boosters do at a fraction of the cost.

## Nutrient mixing order (critical)

**Never mix Part A and Part B concentrates directly.** Calcium (Part B) + phosphorus (Part A) = calcium phosphate precipitate. Ruins the nutrients and clogs equipment.

In the mixing bucket, always follow this order:

1. Add water to the bucket (auto-fill or manual).
2. Add CalMag if needed (manual dose). Stir.
3. Add Part A. Stir.
4. Add Part B. Stir.
5. Check pH. Adjust with pH Down (or rarely pH Up).
6. Check EC/TDS. Verify concentration is on target.

## Feeding schedule by stage

### Seedling (weeks 1-2)

- EC target: 0.4-0.8 (250-400 ppm)
- pH: 5.8-6.0
- Frequency: Light misting or small hand-water. Keep coco moist but not soaked.
- Nutrients: Very light Part A + B at ~25-50% of veg strength. CalMag at half rate.

### Early veg (weeks 2-4)

- EC target: 0.8-1.4 (500-700 ppm)
- pH: 5.8-6.0
- Frequency: 1x per day
- Nutrients: Part A + Part B at full veg strength. CalMag at full rate.

### Late veg (weeks 4-6)

- EC target: 1.4-1.8 (700-900 ppm)
- pH: 5.8-6.0
- Frequency: 1-2x per day
- Nutrients: Part A + Part B at increasing strength. CalMag at full rate.

### Transition / early flower (weeks 1-3 of 12/12)

- EC target: 1.6-2.0 (800-1000 ppm)
- pH: 5.8-6.2
- Frequency: 1-2x per day
- Nutrients: Part A (with MKP mixed in) + Part B. CalMag at full rate.
- Start adding MKP to Tank A at transition ratios.

### Mid flower (weeks 3-5)

- EC target: 2.0-2.4 (1000-1200 ppm)
- pH: 5.8-6.2
- Frequency: 2-3x per day
- Nutrients: Part A (with MKP + K2SO4) + Part B. CalMag at full rate.
- Peak feeding. Watch for tip burn (back off EC if leaves claw or tip burn appears).

### Late flower (weeks 5-8)

- EC target: 1.6-2.0 (800-1000 ppm)
- pH: 5.8-6.2
- Frequency: 2x per day
- Nutrients: Reduce Part A and Part B. Keep K2SO4 high. Reduce or stop MKP.
- Watch for fade. Reduce N (less Part B) as plants natural yellow.

### Flush (last 7-10 days)

- EC target: 0.0 (plain water only)
- pH: 5.8-6.2
- Frequency: 2x per day
- No nutrients. Plain pH-adjusted water only.
- Some growers add a tiny amount of CalMag during flush.

## Feeding frequency

Coco drains fast and holds less water than soil. Plants in coco need more frequent watering than soil plants.

| Stage | Frequency | Volume per feeding |
|---|---|---|
| Seedling | Mist / small hand water | Just keep moist |
| Early veg | 1x per day | Until ~10% runoff |
| Late veg | 1-2x per day | Until ~10-15% runoff |
| Early flower | 1-2x per day | Until ~15-20% runoff |
| Mid-late flower | 2-3x per day | Until ~15-20% runoff |
| Flush | 2x per day | Heavy runoff |

### Why frequent feeding

- Coco does not hold nutrients the way soil does. It releases them.
- Frequent light feedings maintain a steady nutrient availability at the root zone.
- Less risk of salt buildup between feedings.
- The automated dosing system makes frequent feeding easy.

## Runoff management

- **Target 10-20% runoff each feeding.** If you pour in 1L, expect 100-200mL to drain out.
- **Measure runoff EC periodically.** If runoff EC is much higher than input EC, salts are building up. Increase runoff percentage or add a plain-water flush between feedings.
- **If runoff EC is lower than input**: plants are taking up nutrients aggressively. This is normal in early flower. Keep feeding.
- **If runoff EC is much higher than input**: salt accumulation. Flush with plain pH'd water at 2-3x normal volume, then resume feeding.

## Mother liquor recipes

Prepare concentrated stock solutions for the peristaltic pump system. Pre-mix supplements into Tank A at ratios for the current growth stage.

### Concentrated stock approach

A common approach: dissolve nutrients at 100-250x the target ppm per mL, then use timed peristaltic pump doses to hit the target.

Example for a 10L mixing bucket:
- If Tank A concentrate is 200x strength, you need ~50mL per 10L to hit target.
- Calibrate each pump: measure mL delivered per 10 seconds, calculate run time.

### Tank A recipes (per 1L concentrate, adjust to taste)

**Veg Tank A**: Mega Crop Part A only (follow label rate for concentrate)
- Dissolve Part A at concentrated rate per label directions.
- Add small amount of Epsom Salt (~1g/L of concentrate).

**Early flower Tank A**: Part A + MKP
- Dissolve Part A at concentrated rate.
- Add MKP at ~0.5g/L of concentrate.

**Late flower Tank A**: Part A + MKP + K2SO4
- Dissolve Part A at concentrated rate (possibly reduced).
- Add MKP at ~0.3g/L.
- Add K2SO4 at ~1g/L.

Always dissolve each powder fully before adding the next. Use warm water if needed.

### Tank B

- Mega Crop Part B only. Do not add anything to Tank B unless you have verified it contains no phosphorus or sulfur.

### pH Down

- Use as-is from the bottle. Phosphoric acid (common pH Down) at commercial concentration.

## Common coco-specific issues

### Calcium or magnesium lockout

- Symptoms: new growth curling, brown spots on leaves, interveinal chlorosis on lower leaves.
- Cause: Unbuffered coco, or insufficient CalMag.
- Fix: Verify coco was buffered. Increase CalMag. Check pH is in range.

### Potassium toxicity

- Symptoms: Dark green leaves, clawing tips, Ca and Mg deficiency symptoms (K competes with Ca and Mg uptake).
- Cause: Heavy K dosing (MKP + K2SO4) without enough Mg.
- Fix: Add Epsom Salt to the feeding. Reduce K2SO4 if symptoms persist.

### Salt buildup

- Symptoms: Runoff EC climbing, leaf tip burn, slow growth.
- Cause: Insufficient runoff, or going too long between plain-water flushes.
- Fix: Flush with 2-3x volume of plain pH'd water. Increase runoff percentage.

### Overwatering in coco

- Yes, it is possible. Symptoms: slow growth, drooping, yellowing lower leaves.
- Cause: Soggy coco with no air. Usually from pots sitting in runoff, or coco too finely packed.
- Fix: Ensure pots drain freely. Elevate pots off trays. Add more perlite. Increase time between waterings slightly.

### pH drift

- Coco can cause pH to rise over time as it releases cations.
- Check and adjust pH every feeding. The automated pH sensor helps catch drift early.
- If pH keeps rising: more CalMag in the solution, or verify coco was properly buffered.
