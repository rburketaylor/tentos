# Tent Build and Safety

## Cheapest build options

### Buy a tent

- Fastest path for a small grow
- Typical target size in the notes: about `60 x 60 x 140 cm`

### DIY enclosure

- PVC frame if cost and easy assembly matter most
- `2x2` wood frame if you want stronger mounting points
- Panda film is the best cheap interior choice from the archived notes
- Black plastic, canvas, blackout curtain, or moving blanket can handle the exterior light blocking

## Material guidance

- Panda film is usually easier and more durable than Mylar.
- Mylar is usable, but it creases easily and is more annoying to work with.
- A simple flap door with Velcro or similar closure is enough for a budget build.

## Safety priorities

- Modern LED grows are mostly electrical risk, not HID-style heat risk.
- Secure the light so it cannot fall onto fabric or film.
- Keep reflective film and plastic away from the fixture heatsink and wiring.
- Avoid overloaded power strips, bad extension cords, and questionable drivers.
- Maintain airflow even in a DIY enclosure.

## Light value notes

- Avoid fake wattage marketing.
- Old blurple fixtures are usually a poor value now.
- A single honest board is the cheapest clean solution for a `2x2` or small `3x3`.
- Two smaller boards can be a useful spacing compromise if one panel gives uneven coverage.
- Bar or segmented lights matter more once the footprint gets larger.
